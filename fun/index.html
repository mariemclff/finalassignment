Why Norway?

Our Top 4 of locations to visit in Norway

On this page you'll be presented to the wonders of the Troll's country, including:

  • The breathtaking Preikestolen
  • The infamous Lofoten islands
  • The magic of Tromsø
  • The disconnected land of Svalbard
  • Let's discover

Marie's pick

The hike to Preikestolen was one of the most incredible experiences I had in Norway! The two hours walk was exhausting but the view at the end was completely worth it. From this rock, you have a breathtaking sigth on the endless fjord and beautiful norwegian nature. I would definitely recommend experimenting this during the summer.

Nathan's pick

My trip to the aurora capital was probably one of the most amazing experiences of my life! If you want to live an incredible adventure in Norway, I absolutely advise you to visit Tromsø! It's true, the sun sets early, but we visited many museums went it got dark so it wasn't a problem. And the arctic cathedral is even more beautiful at night! And don't even get me started on the Northern Lights, which are magical, and I would probably remember it my whole life.

Sander's pick

The westernmost island on the Lofoten, Moskenesøy, offers the most famous trails with several adventure companies and walking guides based in and around Reine. While Tromsø and Alta steal the headlines for aurora spotting in Norway, the lack of artificial light makes the Lofoten islands a well worth alternative. Lastly, the Lofoten islands are perfect for road trips, just hop in a car and take in the athmosphere.

Irina's pick

If you want to go to the end of the world, Svalbard is the place you were searching for. Few people, virgin nature, glaciers, home to a lot of ice bear families and guides wearing weapons all over the place… this is the best place to go if you want to disconnect from reality!
